Do you have a separation agreement or a court order that sets out what you've agreed to or what the court has already ordered about parenting, child and spousal support, and/or property?

Refer to that separation agreement or court order when filling out the documents for divorce. You'll also need to attach a copy to the documents you file with the court.

The parts of the agreement that deal with parenting and support can be enforced as if they were in a court order.

More information on how to flle your agreement can be found on the Legal Services Society web site.

When you divorce or separate from your spouse or common-law partner, there are many issues you may need work out. These include:

  • How will you take care of the children?
  • How will you divide property and debt?
  • Will one of you pay child support?
  • Will one of you pay spousal support?

If you and your spouse can work together to reach a fair agreement, it's important that you have it written down and that both of you sign it.

More information on how to flle your agreement can be found on the Legal Services Society web site.

If you need to get a separation agreement, contact a family justice counsellor, mediator, or family law lawyer for help.

The Legal Services Society web site has a number of resources to help you to find out how to draft your own agreement:

  • Making an agreement after you separate
  • Who can help you reach an agreement?
  • Self-help guide How to write your own separation agreement

A separation agreement workshop is also available on the Justice Education Society web site.

A separation agreement is only required to be filed or disclosed in a joint divorce action if you have children, in which case it is attached as an exhibit to the Child Support Affidavit. In a sole proceeding, you must disclose this information right in Form F3.
